    SECTORS    

These are some main sectors of the Make in India program:

 AUTOMOBILES AND COMPONENTS

  1. Sixth largest producer in the world with an average annual production of 24 million vehicles in 2016.
  2. India has the fifth largest passenger vehicle and commercial vehicle market.
  3. Presence of four large auto manufacturing hubs across the country: Delhi-Gurgaon-Faridabad in the north, Mumbai-Pune-Nashik-Aurangabad in the west, Chennai- Bengaluru-Hosur in the south and Jamshedpur-Kolkata in the east.
  4. Contributes to 7.1 % of India's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) by volume.
  5. Six million-plus hybrid and electric vehicles to be sold annually, by 2020.Two-wheeler production has grown from 8.5 million units annually to 15.9 million units in the last seven years.
  1. Size of the Indian Auto Component Industry is around USD 39 billion (2015-16) and contributes 2.3% to India's Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
  2. Auto Component Industry registered a Compound Annual Growth Rate(CAGR) of 14% during 2006-16, during the same period exports increased from USD 3.2 billion to USD 10.81 billionIn the last financial year, the auto component exports contributed 4% to India's overall exports.
  3. Surpassed Automotive Mission Plan (AMP) 2016-26 turnover target of USD 31.84 billion in FY 2016 by registering USD 39 billion.
  4. Aims to achieve 5 times growth with a turnover of USD 200 billion, exports targets between USD 70-80 billion and an investment of USD 30-40 billion by 2026.Aftermarket sector growing at a CAGR of 12% annually.

AVIATION

  1. India is the world's fastest growing domestic aviation market, and has posted the fastest full year growth rate for three years in a row now.
  2. India's Revenue Passenger Kilometre (RPK) growth of 17.5% was higher than the global average growth of 7% in 2017.
  3. At USD 16 billion, India's aviation market is currently the 9th largest in the world and is projected to be the 3rd largest by 2020, and largest by 2030.
  4. More than 80 international airlines connecting to over 40 countries.

BIOTECHNOLOGY

  1. India is among the top 12 biotech destinations in the world and ranks third in the Asia Pacific.
  2. India has the second highest number of United States Food & Drug Administration(USFDA) approved plants.
  3. No.1 producer of Hepatitis B vaccine recombinant.
  4. Indian biotech industry shall touch USD 100 billion by 2025.
  5. Large consumer base with increasing disposable income.

CONSTRUCTION

  1. USD 1 Trillion investments for infrastructure sector projected during 2012-17
  2. USD 650 Billion investments in urban infrastructure estimated over next 20 years.
  3. 100% Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) permitted through the automatic route for townships, cities.
  4. Construction sector contributes towards 8% of the Indian GDP (at constant prices). Last five year estimates (2006-07 to 2010-11).
  5. Additional Fact: increased to 3.85 lakh crore (7.9% of the total GDP) in 2010-11 from 284798 crore (8% of the total GDP) in 2006-07.
  6. Additional Facts: Growth rate for GDP in construction 8.1%
  7. 100 Smart Cities and 500 AMRUT Cities will invite investment of 2 Trillion Rupees in the next five years.
  8. INR 62,009 Crore. would be invested under Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) in urban areas.

DEFENCE MANUFACTURING

  1. India has the third largest armed forces in the world.
  2. The allocation of Defence in the India's union budget is approx USD 34.53 billion and 31.1% of the defence budget is spent on capital acquisitions.
  3. 60% of defence related requirements are met by imports which offers a huge opportunity for import substitution.

IT AND ELECTRONICS SYSTEM

​

  1. The electronics market of India is one of the largest in the world and is anticipated to reach USD 400 billion by 2020.
  2. Sector is expected to grow at a projected CAGR of 26% for the period 2014-2020. (CAGR – Cumulative annual growth rate)
  3. India has the 4th Largest Start up ecosystem in the world.
  4. Several government driven initiatives and incentives like – Electronics Development Fund (EDF), Electronics Manufacturing Clusters (EMCs) scheme, National Knowledge Network & National Optical Fibre Network.
  5. 100% FDI is allowed through automatic route (49% through automatic route in strategic defence electronics sector)

 PHARMACEUTICALS

  1. Third largest pharmaceuticals market by 2020 in terms of incremental growth.
  2. 20% of global exports in generics, making it the largest provider of generic medicines globally.
  3. USD 45 Billion in revenue by 2020, revenue of USD 55 billion by 2020 as base case, and can grow to USD 70 billion in a aggressive case scenario.
  4. USD 26.1 Billion in generics by 2016.
  5. USD 200 Billion to be spent on infrastructure by 2024.
  6. India's filing of Drug Master Files's (DMF's) with USFDA as of Dec 2013 is 3411, the highest filed by any country in the world.
  7. Total exports of Drugs, Pharmaceuticals for 2013-14 at USD 15,095 million, recording a growth rate 2.5% over the corresponding period of previous years.

PORTS AND SHIPPING

  1. India has 12 major ports and 64 minor ports handling Export Import cargo.
  2. 20% of global exports in generics, making it the largest provider of generic medicines globally.
  3. The total cargo handling capacity of Major ports during 2016-17 was 1065.83 MTPA.
  4. USD 26.1 Billion in generics by 2016.
  5. In 2016-17, 57 projects worth USD 14 billion created an additional capacity of 103.52 MTPA.
  6. Budget 2018-19 allocation to Ministry of Shipping stands at USD 289 million.

HIGH SPEEED RAILWAY

  1. Indian Railways network spans more than 66030 kms, making it the world’s third largest rail network.
  2. India has the fourth largest rail freight carrier in the world and has the largest passenger carrier.
  3. Indian railways carry around 3.04 million tonnes and 22.4 million passengers of freight every day.
  4. 1.3 million strong work force in Indian Railways.
